Overview

Give a high-level overview of what you implemented in this project. Think about what you've built as a whole. Share your thoughts on what interesting things you've learned from completing the project.
In this project, we implemented a simulation where a cloth falls onto an object. This required us to simulate the cloth as a mass and spring system. In Part 1, we implemented the springs and then implemented three constraints, structural, shearing, and bending. In part 2, we then caclultd the force on each mass using Hooke's law and also weakened the force for the bending constraint. We then used Verlet integration to simulate motion of each point mass which takes into account the position, velocity, and acceleration due to all forces. In part 3, we handled coliisions with a sphere and a plane, where we move the object to the surface of the object, if it intersects the sphere or goes through the plane. In part 4, we handle self collisions using spatial hasing. At each timestep, we check all the point masses in the same 3D space using a hash and then check whether they are close together and then apply a correction vector to each point mass based on this information. Finally in part 5, we write a few vertex and fragment shaders. We first implement diffuse shading for diffuse lighting and then combine it with ambient and specular shading for the Blinn-Phong shader model. Then we come up with texture mapping that takes a texture and applies it to the object. We then implement bump mapping which modifies the normal vectors to create bumps on an object. it does this by applying a tangent-bitangent-normal matrix and using a height map which can be the r component of the color component of the texture at that uv position. We then use this fragment shader and combine it with a displacement vertex shader which displaces the vertex positions in the direction of the normal vector at a value proportional to the height map to be consistent with the new geometry. This will be the displacement shader. Finally, we implemnted a mirror shader using environment mapping using the normal vector.

Describe the effects of changing the spring constant ks; how does the cloth behave from start to rest with a very low ks? A high ks?

For a very low ks, it seems like the cloth is much more (for lack of a better word) "wiggly", and it seems to behave almost like a sheet of what I would would describe as "as close to liquid as jello can get". If it were made of a bunch of tiny little floppy pen springs that were stretched far past their proper length and stiched together in a manner like a chain link fence into a sheet of paper, that's how it moves.

For a very high ks, I would describe it as being much more "stiff" almost like a piece of plastic packaging or thick construction paper or maybe a towel falling rather than something a lot more loose like the previous one. Basically not jello like and a lot more rigid. This can be seen from the formula F = kx. Because kx is inversely proportional, the lower k, the larger displacement which leads to the cloth to be more wiggly. Increasing k limits the displacement and thus leads to stiffness. It falls evenly compared to low spring constant which wiggles around.

What about for density?

Changing the density I would say changes how much the cloth "droops in on itself" at the top, like how much it caves in on itself. High density yields a lot less support like a wet towel, while lower densities tend to hold themselves up more like a piece of paper and have a lot less "give" to their structure. This can kind of be seen because the graviational force on the cloth is larger due to increased mass. F = kx -> x = F/k so there will be a larger displacement and as such can cave in and droop at increased densities.

What about for damping?

When I set the damping to zero it seems to create a perpetual motion machine where the cloth or floppy substance doesn't ever seem to stop swaying back and forth, which I find mildly amusing, which also makes sense since it never looses energy or tends to zero. Its movements remind me of something like rubber gloves or what could be described as a thin rubber sheet of some kind that is flapping about, which I'd like to think makes sense because I'd guess that rubber is fairly elastic while also not being terribly light so its movements would resemble something with a rather low damping factor. With less damping there is less loss of energy, and as such the cloth moves more and more and "wiggles" as damping decreases due to kinetic energy still being in the system.

Setting damping to the high side on the other hand reminds me of something like holding a sheet of aluminium foil on both corners and letting it slowly fall, basically it falls very slowly and with almost zero rebound or bounce in it's step. Again, I'd like to think this is the case because of the materials that it makes me think of aluminium being not super elastic or having a rather high damping factor because it's quite malleable and therefore absorbs or translates all of the potential gravitational energy into deformation and heat. As such, it will move less and less.

Part 3: Handling collisions with other objects

In part 3, we handled coliisions with a sphere and a plane, where we move the object to the surface of the object, if it intersects the sphere or goes through the plane. For the sphere, we calculate the position from the origin and checked if the magnitude was less than the origin. We then computed the direction and multiplied by the radius to calculate the tangent point. We then calculated the distacne from the tangent point from the last point and multiplied this by 1-f for friction adn then added it to the last position which should bring the new position close to the tangent point so it doesn't go through the sphere. The plane collision was more difficult to do. To calculate if the mass would go through the plane, we calculate the vector from a point on the plane to the last position and take the dot product of it with the normal vector of the plane. We do the same thing with the current position. If the product of these 2 is negative, then we know the point mass went through the plane. We then get the last position dot product and divide it by the difference of the two dot products and then multiply it by the vector connecting the last position and the current position and add it to the last position to get the tangent point(the point it intersects the plane.) We then add the SURFCE_OFFSET to the tangent point and then get the vector from the last position to this point and then multiply it by (1-f) for friction and add this vector the last position as the correction vector to get the new point.

Explain in your own words what is a shader program and how vertex and fragment shaders work together to create lighting and material effects.

From the spec and lecture shader programs are programs that run in parallel on GPU, take an input, and output a 4d vector, in our case, color. They execute sections of the graphics pipeline. Vertex shaders apply transformations to vertices and use the position and normal vectors to compute something and then write the final vertex position. Fragment shaders take in geometric attributes of the fragment calculated from vertex shader to compute a color. For example, in Phong shading you interpolate the average normal at the vertex of each frament across each pixel and then compute the normal at each pixel.

Explain the Blinn-Phong shading model in your own words. Show a screenshot of your Blinn-Phong shader outputting only the ambient component, a screen shot only outputting the diffuse component, a screen shot only outputting the specular component, and one using the entire Blinn-Phong model.

Blinn-phong model includes specular shading which describes how intensity depends on view direction. It calcualtes the half vector between the light and your viewing direction. If this vector aligns with the normal of the surface, the intensity is max because it's "like a mirror reflection" and decreases the more disalligned the normal is from the half vector. The shading model also includes ambient and diffuse shading.

Explain what you did in your custom shader, if you made one.

For the shader I implemented chromatic aberration which can be read about in this wikipedia link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromatic_aberration. To do this implementation I modified the texture.frag file to implement lateral chromatic abberation meaning that I varied the u coordinates. Because blue is refracted more than red, I sampled the texture for the blue color at a smaller u value for the input uv and I sampled red at a larger u value and green at the normal value. This leads to dispersive effects and a more blurry image. I can also vary the offset of which u to sample.
